Opportunities

There are many opportunities to make smartphones more useable for the elderly, for example, changing interfaces to accommodate users who might have a hard time seeing. This could be implemented through changes such as larger text sizes, the option for text-to-speech, and simplification of navigation.

Ideally, there would be smartphones that are designed for the elderly however, as of now there are no mainstream smartphones that fit the criteria. A few good options in the current range of smartphones include the Nokia 3.4 or the Google Pixel 7, which we will talk a bit more about in the choices section.

Digital literacy training for the elderly specifically, using adapted interfaces would help them to understand the advantages of having a smartphone, as well as how to navigate through it, and the essential apps. This could be implemented at local libraries as part of their educational classes, spots frequented by the elderly, such as community centers or online tutorials/website and tech/instructor-based training programs. This support, especially when continuous, will build confidence in elder’s technology illiteracy and give them the essential skills in today’s digital climate.

Laptop checkout programs developed by local libraries are an example of an opportunity for the elderly to adapt to technology. Libraries introducing digital technology workshops and classes that provide hyperlinks to trustworthy web resources for finding medical and health-related information such as medlineplus.gov can be a big help in those elderly who are struggling in gathering important information. In addition, assisting in the decision-making process for health and medical-related issues. This decrease computer anxiety the elderly may have and increase computer interest to improve their technology illiteracy (Xie B & Bugg J, 2009).

Online tutorials/website opportunities such as Senior Planet operated by OATS provides digital literacy training for the elderly. Senior Planet prepares weekly classes that teaches how to conduct internet research using tactics including analyzing websites, determining the source of the material and finding tools for fact-checking as elderly are easily troubled by fake news. This motivates elderly to stay away from confirmation bias traps and encourages independent thought and cognitive growth (Nash S,2019).

Instructor-based training is a great opportunity as it includes task performance, social relationships and increase elderly well-being, In-person settings and incorporates interaction and socialization with instructors and fellow senior learners. Instructor-based training for seniors can form significant social ties that are vital to their wellbeing and social support. Because of the group settings, which can lessen the sense of isolation, this advances their sense of confidence and inclusion. Additionally, the social contacts that take place during computer training will contribute to the beneficial relationship between technology training and elderly well-being. (Xie B & Bugg J, 2009) As they share their experiences with overcoming difficulties, other elderly people become more motivated to learn about technology to become efficient at using digital tools.

Assisting the elderly to understand a wide range of opportunities the internet or community has to offer and encourage elderly with guidance, will provide them reassurance in adapting with digital technology as adopting to technology is a slow process for elderly, patience is needed therefore these opportunities are beneficial among consistent support and willingness.
