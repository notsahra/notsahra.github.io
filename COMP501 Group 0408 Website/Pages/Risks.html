Risks

Being elderly with poor technology literacy skills means there will be missed opportunities, as the world we live in is gradually going digital. Many necessary services are now available online. Without the ability to use these digital tools, the elderly will have difficulty accessing and are thus excluded from these key resources which include banking online, medical gateways, services provided by the government, shopping online, and more (Akinola.S, 2021).

Technology is being used more and more for social contact and communication. Senior citizens who lack technology literacy will be unable to stay in touch with friends and family using digital technologies, which can add to a sense of isolation because they are cut off from the ties they most long for (Conger.k & Griffith.E, 2020).

Social responsibility towards the elderly who don't adapt to modern day technology is a risk. They stress that technology will substitute in person conversation and increase the feeling of loneliness. They enjoy traditional methods of engaging communication to the community such as face to face contact or involvement in daily errands. They fear technology services will take opportunities such as business and jobs from important institutions like banks if they don't physically go themselves. The elderly reject modern day technology out of guilt because it will harm their social ties (Lancaster University, 2018).

The elderly are vulnerable to digital fraud, due to their low level of technology literacy and an inability to secure important information. Low level of financial literacy is regularly found among the elderly. This leads to them having difficulties recognizing fraud when encountering it. Looking at how the financial system has grown over time, the elderly lack the digital literacy training necessary to overcome these obstacles. Technology improvements and the digitalization of financial services might provide challenges for elderly people who are not as comfortable or accustomed to accessing online resources. Social isolation serves a significant risk aspect of fraud vulnerability, especially for senior citizens who tend to be lonelier. Scammers will pretend to feel sympathy for the elder’s issues, which manipulates the elder’s emotions and creates trust, which later leads to scams. Elderly people who are socially isolated have a strong psychological need to interact with different people, often complete strangers, makes them easier targets for scam artists. Psychological vulnerability is also leading to the effects of social isolation and identified inadequate stratification of social desires. Over time we can estimate future incidents of fraud, especially vulnerable elderly, who are twice as likely to be taken advantage of. Lichtenberg et al. 2016 focuses on the significance of taking psychological vulnerability into account when calculating fraud susceptibility. Elderly focus more on the false benefits that the scam artists claim them to be, thus resulting in the elderly to feel good as they analyze the fake data. They are prone to disregard the unlikely factors of the fraud situation that could trigger concerns (Shao.J, Zhang.Q, Ren.Y, Li.X & Lin.T, 2019).

According to total loss data from the FTC's (2020) full year, seen there is a higher percentage of younger groups fraud lost, there is a higher amounts of fraud loss for elderly. In addition, the age group 60 and over reported losses of $436 to $1300 points up how elderly are easy targets to digital fraud for example in the pandemic era.

According to Federal Trade Commission (FTC) data (2019), online contact was the main method of scamming elderly people in general. Age 70 and older, phone calls were mostly used contact method targeting the elderly.
