Technology/Topic and their Impact on Society

In a world where smartphones are an essential tool in our lives, we must remember that this is not the case for everyone, not everyone has adapted to daily, heavy reliance on technology. While the latest generation has easily transitioned into this new digital world, many seniors still struggle to understand and use smartphones. In today’s increasingly digital world this gap between the elderly and their ability to use smartphones creates this new issue – the elderly’s current smartphone illiteracy is leaving them behind.

The phrase “smartphone illiteracy” describes the challenges that people encounter while trying to understand and use smartphones. Modern smartphones are very different from what elderly people grew up and used for most of their lives, they have many applications, tools and can be intimidating for those who are not used to them. Senior citizens are used to more traditional means of communication and technology, like landline telephones or mailing letters, while mobile phones are extremely different.

The elderly and their current smartphone illiteracy is a topic that really showcases the challenges faced by older generations as they struggle to adapt to rapidly advancing technology. This is particularly relevant today as the world’s population continues to age, the number of the elderly increases. In fact, by 2050, the world population of older people will be around 400 million.

In recent years, the use of technology, especially smartphones, has become prominent. According to statista (2022) by the end of 2020, 78.05% of the world’s population used smartphones. While some senior citizens have become technologically competent, a significant portion still struggles interacting with newer technologies, which means certain elderly people are excluded from some essential services (like healthcare or shopping) and are being left behind in this increasingly digital society.

There are many reasons that the elderly have yet to adapt to modern smartphones – one of these is the design of smartphones. Typically, smartphones are small touchscreen devices that connect users to the internet. These smartphones were not designed with the elderly in mind, the user interface has small font size which can be difficult for those with vision issues, the touchscreen may be inconvenient for those with dexterity problems, and smartphones have a very complex navigation system, especially for new users.

Another determinant for smartphone illiteracy among senior citizens is the rapid change in technology. Technology is evolving at an extremely fast pace, with new smartphones coming out every year, there is an overwhelming amount of constant learning to not fall behind. This continuously changing environment is demanding, and it takes effort and time to learn the newest software updates – not to mention the cost of upgrading your phone, which may not seem important for those who don’t fully understand the use of smartphones.

Finally, the elderly have had less exposure and training compared to younger generations. While Gen Z and Gen Alpha been brought up with technology ingrained in their education and lives, the older generations have not. There is a lack of training programs on how to use technology, it is just assumed that everyone will know how to use it. Without proper guidance and support there is little opportunities for the elderly to learn and adapt to new technologies.

Overall, the current smartphone illiteracy in the elderly is a topic that has an impact on today’s technologically advanced society. Senior citizens face some challenges in the way of becoming smartphone literate, like design limitations, constant change in technology, and lack of exposure and training. Acknowledging and learning the importance of smartphone literacy is so important in todays society and by addressing the issues the elderly confront, we can create a more inclusive technological society for all generations.
